Privacy Policy

Your privacy is important to us. This Privacy Notice describes the information we collect and how we use that information. By using this website, you consent to the terms of this Privacy Notice. This Privacy Notice is effective as of January 1, 2020.

PROTECTING THE PRIVACY OF YOUR PERSONAL INFORMATION

We are committed to protecting your privacy and to implementing technology that gives you a secure online experience. We maintain physical, electronic and procedural safeguards to transmit and store information about you. For instance, we may use computer virus protection software, and firewalls for data transmission. We routinely evaluate security technology and techniques to protect your information.

INFORMATION WE COLLECT ABOUT YOU

We collect "nonpublic personal information" about you from several sources:

  • Information you provide to us when you visit any of our websites, such as through online forms, registration forms, surveys and other entries, which may include information such as your name, address, driver's license number or other government-issued identification number, Social Security number, and e-mail address;
  • Information about your transactions with us, our vendors or others, such as payment history and loan balances;
  • Information we receive from third parties, such as other lenders, regarding creditworthiness and credit history;
  • Information from consumer reporting agencies, such as information contained in your consumer report; and
  • When you click on one of our ads at a third-party site, we may collect the URL of the site where that ad was displayed.

We also collect certain non-personal, anonymous information about your use of our site such as information about your computer, including your IP address, the type of browser you are using, and your computer's operating system. Except as otherwise explained in this Privacy Notice or as may be prohibited by law, we may combine this kind of anonymous information with your personal information.

WEBSITE TRACKING TECHNOLOGY

To help ensure we are publishing content users need and want, we collect aggregated site visitation statistics using cookies. When someone visits the Web site, a cookie is placed on the visitor's machine (if the visitor accepts cookies). If you choose not to have your browser accept cookies from our Web site, you will not be able to subscribe to the service offerings on the Web site. On certain of our sites, we deploy a Google Analytics cookie that provides website statistics and web traffic data. Website visitors that do not want their visit data reported by Google Analytics can install the Google Analytics opt-out browser add-on. Please click here for more information. You can also manage your settings for this cookie and opt-out of this feature by visiting the Google Ads Settings page. We also deploy another cookie on sites where we have customer service chat functionality, called Provide Support. If you do not want to allow information to be collected through the use of this cookie, you can generally opt out of providing this information by setting your browser to reject cookies. However, please be aware that you may not be able to access or use some or all features or areas of our websites if you have disabled the use of cookies.

HOW WE USE AND DISCLOSE COLLECTED PERSONALLY-IDENTIFIABLE INFORMATION

Except as described below, we do not sell, trade, or otherwise transfer your personally identifiable information to outside parties without your consent. We may disclose your personally identifiable information to trusted third parties who assist us in operating our website, processing transactions, conducting our business, or servicing you, so long as these parties agree to keep this information confidential, consistent with this privacy notice. In particular, the information we collect about you is primarily used to connect you with our vendors and contact you regarding administrative notices, Web site updates, upcoming events, or products and services offered by our vendors. We also may use your information to resolve disputes, troubleshoot problems, enforce our agreements with you (including but not limited to our Website Terms and Conditions), as well as for direct marketing purposes.

YOUR CALIFORNIA PRIVACY RIGHTS

The California Consumer Protection Act (“CCPA”) provides California residents with specific rights concerning their personal information. Simple Fast Loans, Inc. (alternatively referred to as the “Company”, “we” or “us”) is committed to protecting your rights under the CCPA. This section describes your CCPA rights, how you may exercise those rights, and the Company’s obligations in response.

NOTICE OF COLLECTION:

Categories of personal information we collect about you:

The Company collects some or all of the following categories of personal information about its California consumers, and for the stated reasons, as described below:

  • Your name, driver’s license number, social security number, and other identifying information in order to confirm your identity;
  • Your address, phone number, email address, geo-location data and related information, in order to confirm your identifying information and residence, and in order to contact you during the loan application process and the term of any loan offered to you;
  • Income information, to evaluate your ability to repay any loan offered to you;
  • Employment information, to help us confirm the source of your income;
  • Information regarding your credit worthiness, to evaluate your willingness to repay any loan offered to you;
  • Your military status, in order to comply with the Military Lending Act and related state laws;
  • Your banking account information, including debit card information in certain circumstances, to allow you to conveniently pay your loan through our payment portal or through a pre-authorized electronic funds transfer.

SOURCES OF THE CATEGORIES OF PERSONAL INFORMATION

Our primary source for the information listed above is you. We collect most of the information from you at the time you apply for one of our loan products.

In some cases, we may collect personal information about you from third parties. Third parties from whom we collect your personal information may include lead generators, our own company affiliates, or consumer reporting agencies.

In some cases, we may share (but not sell) your personal information to third parties. Third parties with whom we share your personal information may include lead generators, our own company affiliates, consumer reporting agencies, our banking partners, and governmental entities that routinely examine our business practices.

In responding to requests for access, the Company will use reasonable methods for verifying that you, as the individual making the request for access, are the consumer about whom the Company has collected information.   • Your request demands disclosure of a Social Security number, driver’s license number, other government-issued identification number, an account number, an account password, or security questions and answers;
  • Due to a conflict with other state or federal law.

If your request to delete is granted due to the absence of an exception, the Company will separately confirm your request before doing so.

In the event the Company changes its practices in this regard, the Company will update this policy and provide an avenue for you and other consumers to opt-out of such sales.
